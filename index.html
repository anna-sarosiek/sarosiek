About

I work between philosophy, biosemiotics, and cognitive science, tracing how meaning grows in systems that sense, adapt, and transform. I study signs in life—from ants mapping invisible paths to moss reading humidity—and ask what it means for intelligence to emerge without intention. work at the intersection of cognitive science, philosophy, and artificial intelligence. My research focuses on biosemiotics, homeostasis, and adaptive systems that self-regulate and evolve.

This site collects teaching materials, research notes, and reflections linking theory and practice.
